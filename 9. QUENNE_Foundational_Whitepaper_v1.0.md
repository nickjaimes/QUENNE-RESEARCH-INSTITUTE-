Abstract

As artificial intelligence systems approach increasing levels of autonomy, existing architectures reveal critical limitations: instability under uncertainty, excessive energy consumption, fragmented cognition across substrates, and a lack of intrinsic governance. This whitepaper introduces QUENNE (Quantum-Edge-Neuromorphic Engine), a foundational architecture designed to address these limitations at the structural level.

QUENNE establishes regulated autonomy as a first-class system property by integrating quantum probabilistic reasoning, neuromorphic continuous learning, real-time edge embodiment, and bio-inspired homeostatic regulation. Rather than optimizing intelligence in isolation, QUENNE defines a governing doctrine in which capability is constrained by resilience, ethics, and coherence.

This document presents the philosophical foundation, architectural design, governance model, and research roadmap of QUENNE, positioning it as a long-lived reference framework for safe and resilient autonomous systems.

⸻

1. Introduction

1.1 The Problem of Unregulated Intelligence

Modern AI development has focused primarily on:
   •   model scale
   •   performance benchmarks
   •   task-specific optimization

While these advances have produced impressive results, they expose systemic weaknesses:
   •   brittle behavior outside training distributions
   •   escalating energy demands
   •   unsafe emergent actions
   •   fragmented deployment across cloud, edge, and devices

Autonomy without regulation risks instability at both technical and societal levels.

⸻

1.2 The Need for a Foundational Architecture

True autonomy requires more than intelligence. It requires:
   •   governance
   •   stability
   •   adaptation under uncertainty
   •   long-term coherence

These properties cannot be reliably added post hoc.
They must be architectural invariants.

⸻

2. The QUENNE Principle

2.1 The Law of Regulated Intelligence

QUENNE is governed by a single foundational law:

Intelligence without regulation becomes instability.
Regulation without intelligence becomes stagnation.
Sustainable autonomy requires both.

This law defines all design, governance, and deployment decisions within QUENNE.

⸻

2.2 Architecture Over Models

QUENNE is not a model, algorithm, or product.

It is:
   •   an architectural doctrine
   •   a reference system
   •   a governance framework

Models may change.
Architecture endures.

   •   Stallion (decisive strength)
   •   Ant (distributed cooperation)
   •   Crow (strategic foresight)

5.4 EAGLE EYE

A non-acting oversight intelligence providing:
   •   horizon scanning
   •   risk trajectory modeling
   •   strategic constraints

	2.	Ethics & Resilience Constraints
	3.	Strategic Oversight (EAGLE EYE)
	4.	Homeostatic Regulation
	5.	Cognitive Execution
	6.	Behavioral Expression

No subsystem may override doctrine.
