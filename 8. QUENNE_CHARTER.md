Charter of the QUENNE Research Institute

For the Stewardship of Regulated Autonomous Intelligence

Charter Version: 1.0
Date: 2026
Jurisdiction: Japan (International Scope)
Institute Name: QUENNE Research Institute

⸻

Article I — Establishment

The QUENNE Research Institute (“the Institute”) is hereby established as an independent, non-profit, research-based institution dedicated to the advancement, stewardship, and governance of regulated autonomous intelligence.

The Institute operates for the public benefit and the long-term interests of human civilization.

⸻

Article II — Purpose

The purpose of the Institute is to:
	1.	Define and maintain the foundational principles governing autonomous intelligence
	2.	Translate these principles into executable architectures and reference systems
	3.	Ensure that advanced intelligence remains stable, ethical, resilient, and aligned
	4.	Promote open, reproducible, and internationally collaborative research
	5.	Serve as a neutral authority guiding the safe evolution of intelligent systems

⸻

Article III — Guiding Doctrine

The Institute is governed by the Law of Regulated Intelligence, which states:

Intelligence without regulation leads to instability.
Regulation without intelligence leads to stagnation.
Sustainable autonomy requires both.

All research, architecture, and governance decisions of the Institute shall comply with this law.

⸻

Article IV — Core Principles

The Institute shall operate according to the following immutable principles:
	1.	Regulation First — Stability and governance precede capability
	2.	Resilience by Design — Systems must degrade gracefully under stress
	3.	Architectural Alignment — Safety and ethics are structural, not optional
	4.	Substrate Independence — Principles transcend hardware and models
	5.	Open Science — Research must be transparent and reproducible
	6.	Human-Centered Stewardship — Technology serves civilization, not the reverse

⸻

Article V — Scope of Work

The Institute’s scope includes, but is not limited to:
   •   Cognitive architecture design
   •   Quantum, neuromorphic, and edge intelligence research
   •   Homeostatic and resilience-based AI systems
   •   Autonomous systems governance
   •   Benchmarking and validation of intelligent systems
   •   Development of reference architectures and standards

The Institute does not engage in commercial product development.

⸻

Article VI — Organizational Structure

The Institute shall maintain a lean, principle-driven structure comprising:

1. Doctrine Council

Responsible for maintaining and interpreting the Institute’s guiding doctrine.
Holds veto authority over actions that violate core principles.

2. Architecture Board

Responsible for translating doctrine into technical architectures and ensuring compliance.

3. Research Fellows Network

A global, contribution-based body of researchers and practitioners supporting the Institute’s mission.

⸻

Article VII — Authority & Decision Flow

Authority within the Institute flows in the following order:
	1.	Charter & Doctrine
	2.	Ethics and Resilience Constraints
	3.	Strategic Oversight
	4.	Architectural Governance
	5.	Research Execution

No lower authority may override a higher one.

⸻

Article VIII — Ethics & Safety

All Institute-affiliated research must:
   •   Prioritize safety and stability
   •   Avoid uncontrolled autonomous deployment
   •   Remain auditable and interpretable
   •   Respect human dignity, life, and societal systems

The Institute reserves the right to refuse collaboration that violates these principles.

⸻

Article IX — Funding & Independence

The Institute may receive funding from:
   •   Public research agencies
   •   Philanthropic organizations
   •   International research consortia
   •   Non-controlling industry partnerships

No funding arrangement may compromise the Institute’s independence or doctrine.

⸻

Article X — Intellectual Property

The Institute favors open and permissive licensing to encourage collaboration and adoption, while allowing compatible licensing where necessary.

Doctrine and governance documents remain non-proprietary and publicly accessible.

⸻

Article XI — Founder & Stewardship

The Institute recognizes the role of Founder & Doctrine Steward, responsible for:
   •   Preserving doctrinal coherence
   •   Guiding long-term vision
   •   Ensuring institutional integrity

This role is custodial, not commercial.

⸻

Article XII — Amendments

This Charter may be amended only through:
	1.	Formal review by the Doctrine Council
	2.	Demonstrated necessity for long-term coherence
	3.	Preservation of all core principles

No amendment may violate the Law of Regulated Intelligence.

⸻

Article XIII — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, all remaining assets and intellectual materials shall be transferred to organizations aligned with the Institute’s mission of ethical, resilient intelligence research.

⸻

Closing Declaration

The QUENNE Research Institute exists to ensure that as intelligence becomes autonomous, it remains regulated, resilient, and worthy of trust.
