Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)

QUENNE International Research Consortium (QIRC)

MoU Version: 1.0
Effective Date: 2026
Lead Institution: QUENNE Research Institute (Japan)
Scope: International

⸻

1. Parties

This Memorandum of Understanding (“MoU”) is entered into between:
   •   QUENNE Research Institute, Saitama, Japan
(hereafter referred to as “the Lead Steward”)

and
   •   Participating Institutions, including but not limited to universities, public research laboratories, and non-profit research organizations
(hereafter referred to individually as a “Member” and collectively as “Members”)

⸻

2. Purpose

The purpose of this MoU is to establish a framework for collaboration under the QUENNE International Research Consortium (QIRC), a global research alliance dedicated to the scientific advancement and governance of regulated autonomous intelligence.

This MoU defines shared principles, responsibilities, and expectations while preserving the independence and sovereignty of each Member.

⸻

3. Guiding Principles

All activities conducted under this MoU shall adhere to the following principles:
	1.	Regulated Autonomy — Autonomous intelligence must be governed by explicit architectural constraints.
	2.	Scientific Integrity — Research shall be evidence-based, reproducible, and transparent.
	3.	Open Collaboration — Knowledge sharing and joint publication are encouraged.
	4.	Ethical Responsibility — Safety, resilience, and societal impact take precedence over capability maximization.
	5.	Non-Exclusivity — Participation does not restrict Members from engaging in other collaborations.

⸻

4. Scope of Collaboration

Collaboration under this MoU may include, but is not limited to:
   •   Joint research projects aligned with the QUENNE Doctrine
   •   Development and evaluation of reference architectures
   •   Shared benchmarking and validation studies
   •   Exchange of researchers, fellows, or visiting scholars
   •   Co-authored publications and technical reports
   •   Workshops, symposia, and working groups

Specific activities shall be defined in separate project agreements or addenda.

⸻

5. Roles & Responsibilities

5.1 QUENNE Research Institute (Lead Steward)

The Lead Steward shall:
   •   Maintain and steward the QUENNE Doctrine and foundational principles
   •   Coordinate consortium-wide architectural alignment
   •   Facilitate communication and collaboration among Members
   •   Ensure that consortium activities remain non-commercial and neutral
   •   Provide reference documentation and governance guidance

⸻

5.2 Consortium Members

Each Member shall:
   •   Conduct research aligned with the consortium’s guiding principles
   •   Contribute expertise, data, or resources as agreed per project
   •   Participate in shared evaluation and validation efforts
   •   Respect ethical, safety, and governance requirements
   •   Retain full control over internal operations and personnel

⸻

6. Governance Structure

The Consortium shall operate under a non-hierarchical governance model, consisting of:
   •   Doctrine & Ethics Council — maintains foundational principles
   •   Architecture & Validation Board — coordinates technical coherence
   •   Research Nodes — independent institutions conducting aligned research

No Member shall exercise unilateral control over the Consortium.

⸻

7. Intellectual Property (IP)
	1.	Each Member retains ownership of its background intellectual property.
	2.	Foreground IP arising from joint activities shall be:
      •   jointly owned where applicable, or
      •   licensed under open or permissive terms by mutual agreement.
	3.	The Consortium encourages open science and open publication, subject to applicable laws and institutional policies.
	4.	No proprietary control over foundational doctrine or architecture is permitted.

⸻

8. Publications & Attribution
   •   Joint publications shall acknowledge all contributing Members.
   •   Authorship shall follow standard academic conventions.
   •   The Consortium name (“QUENNE International Research Consortium”) may be cited where appropriate.

⸻

9. Funding & Resources
   •   This MoU does not obligate financial contributions.
   •   Funding may be sought jointly or independently through:
      •   public research grants
      •   international programs
      •   philanthropic funding
   •   No funding arrangement may compromise consortium independence or principles.

⸻

10. Confidentiality

Where necessary, Members may exchange confidential information under separate confidentiality agreements.
This MoU itself does not impose confidentiality obligations.

⸻

11. Duration & Termination
	1.	This MoU shall remain in effect for five (5) years, unless terminated earlier.
	2.	Any Member may withdraw with written notice.
	3.	Withdrawal does not affect rights or obligations accrued prior to termination.

⸻

12. Legal Status

This MoU:
   •   Is a statement of mutual intent
   •   Does not create a legal partnership, joint venture, or agency
   •   Does not impose binding financial obligations

Binding commitments require separate written agreements.

⸻

13. Amendments

This MoU may be amended by mutual written consent of the Lead Steward and participating Members, provided that amendments do not violate the Consortium’s guiding principles.

⸻

14. Governing Law

This MoU shall be interpreted in accordance with international research collaboration norms, with reference to Japanese law where applicable.

⸻
